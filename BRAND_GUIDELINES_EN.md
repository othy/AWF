# AWF — Brand Guidelines

**Version:** 1.0  
**Brand:** AWF — ALLO WORK FORCE  
**Descriptor:** Marketing Workforce  
**Primary market:** Morocco  
**Primary public language:** French  
**Internal documentation language:** English  
**Use cases:** website, social media, client proposals, presentations, ads, reports, content production, design decisions, and future development work.

---

## 1. Brand Platform

### Full name

**ALLO WORK FORCE**

### Visible brand

**AWF**

Use AWF as the main visible brand in the website header, navigation, page titles, social profiles, presentations, commercial documents, and visual identity.

### Descriptor

**Marketing Workforce**

This descriptor is important. It clarifies that AWF is not a recruitment, staffing, outsourcing, or temporary employment company.

AWF is a marketing execution company.

### Primary tagline

**Votre force marketing externalisée.**

Do not translate this tagline in French-first contexts.

### English tagline

**Your outsourced marketing force.**

Use this in English-facing material only.

### Positioning

AWF is a premium outsourced marketing workforce for Moroccan brands that need structured social media, content production, paid media, lead generation, reporting, and marketing operations.

AWF is not a generic social media agency. AWF connects strategy, content, campaigns, conversion, and reporting into one operating system.

### Core promise

**Turn digital attention into concrete business opportunities.**

### Mission

Help Moroccan brands build a clear, consistent, and performance-oriented marketing system that connects content, campaigns, audiences, leads, and business decisions.

### Vision

Become a Moroccan reference for premium marketing operations: a team that thinks like a marketing department, produces like a studio, launches like a growth team, and measures like a performance unit.

## 2. Brand DNA

### What AWF is

AWF is:

- an outsourced marketing workforce;
- a premium execution partner;
- a content, acquisition, conversion, and reporting system;
- a bridge between marketing strategy and field execution;
- a Morocco-first brand;
- a serious alternative to generic social media agencies;
- a marketing production engine, not just a posting service.

### What AWF is not

AWF is not:

- a recruitment company;
- a staffing company;
- a temporary employment agency;
- a cheap community management service;
- a brand that posts just to post;
- an agency that sells empty buzzwords;
- a creative studio with no business logic;
- a cold corporate consultancy;
- an Instagram page pretending to be an agency.

### Brand keywords

- Force
- System
- Clarity
- Execution
- Momentum
- Content
- Conversion
- Performance
- Growth
- Method
- Signal
- Discipline

### Brand principles

1. **Clarity before decoration**  
   Every message must be quickly understandable.

2. **Business before noise**  
   Content must serve a commercial objective, not just fill a calendar.

3. **Method before improvisation**  
   AWF sells a system, not disconnected actions.

4. **Execution before promises**  
   AWF must show how things are built, launched, measured, and improved.

5. **Premium without arrogance**  
   The identity must be modern, sharp, and professional without feeling pretentious.

## 4. Tone of Voice

### General style

AWF speaks in a tone that is:

- clear;
- expert;
- confident;
- structured;
- result-oriented;
- accessible to Moroccan decision-makers;
- professional without being heavy;
- modern without being superficial.

### Writing rule

AWF should sound like a senior marketing team that can explain simply what it does and why it matters.

Avoid language that is too advertising-heavy, vague, motivational, or decorative.

### Good example

> AWF construit un système marketing qui relie contenu, campagnes et conversion.

### Bad example

> Nous boostons votre marque avec des solutions innovantes et créatives.

### Approved French phrases

- Votre contenu doit travailler pour votre business.
- Une présence digitale sans système reste du bruit.
- AWF relie stratégie, contenu, campagnes et conversion.
- Le bon message, au bon format, amplifié au bon moment.
- Nous transformons l’attention en opportunités business.
- La visibilité n’a de valeur que si elle crée du mouvement.
- Un bon contenu ne se contente pas d’être vu. Il guide vers l’action.

### Forbidden phrases

- Boostez votre visibilité.
- Nous donnons vie à vos idées.
- Votre partenaire digital innovant.
- Des solutions créatives adaptées à vos besoins.
- Une communication 360° sur mesure.
- Faites décoller votre marque.
- Votre succès est notre priorité.

## 6. Logo Direction

### Intent

The AWF logo must be premium, typographic, and modular. It should suggest organized force, execution, systems, workflow, and modern marketing operations.

### Recommended direction

- Strong and readable **AWF** monogram.
- Geometric or semi-technical construction.
- Subtle graphic detail suggesting flow, signal, system, or module.
- Balance between business discipline and digital energy.
- Easy adaptation as favicon, social avatar, watermark, and signature.

### Required logo versions

1. **AWF**  
   Short primary version.

2. **AWF — Marketing Workforce**  
   Complete version for website, presentations, and documents.

3. **ALLO WORK FORCE**  
   Institutional/legal version.

4. **Square icon / favicon**  
   Compact version for browser, social profiles, and app icon.

### Strictly avoid

- Phone icon.
- Call-center headset.
- Megaphone.
- Rocket.
- Generic chat bubbles.
- Generic social media icon.
- Cliché growth chart.
- Overly rounded or childish logo.
- Any logo direction that suggests recruitment, staffing, or call centers.

- Use short and strong headlines.
- Avoid long paragraphs.

## 9. Layout System

### General direction

The AWF layout must feel controlled, ordered, and sophisticated.

The website and brand materials should feel like systems, not like a collection of decorative blocks.

### Main rules

- Use a 12-column grid on desktop.
- Use responsive-first layout logic.
- Use generous spacing.
- Build modular sections.
- Use premium cards with thin borders.
- Layer dark surfaces carefully.
- Create clear hierarchy.
- Make CTAs visible but not aggressive.
- Use precise alignment.
- Remove unnecessary decoration.

### Spacing

- Sections must breathe.
- Avoid compressed layouts.
- On mobile, prioritize readability and content order.
- Use fluid spacing where possible.

### Cards

AWF cards should be:

- dark;
- sharp;
- lightly bordered;
- structured;
- readable;
- useful;
- not overly decorative.

## 11. Motion Principles

### Role of animation

AWF animation must help users understand the system. It must not exist only to decorate.

### Allowed motion

- Subtle module reveals.
- Smooth section reveal.
- Light animated signal lines.
- Precise card hover.
- Clean CTA transition.
- Timeline motion that guides reading.

### Forbidden motion

- Slow or heavy animation.
- “Wow effect” without purpose.
- Heavy WebGL without approval.
- Layout shift.
- Excessive parallax.
- Excessive glow.
- Unstable or aggressive hover effects.

### Accessibility

Always respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. The site must remain complete, readable, and functional without animation.

## 12. CTA and Button System

### Principle

AWF must feel premium and professional. CTAs must be clear but not aggressive.

### CTA hierarchy

#### Primary CTA

Use for the main action:

- Demander un audit growth
- Planifier un appel
- Nous contacter
- Lancer un diagnostic

#### Secondary CTA

Use for exploration:

- Découvrir nos services
- Voir nos travaux
- Comprendre notre méthode

#### Tertiary CTA

Use discreetly:

- Envoyer un email
- Appeler
- WhatsApp

### WhatsApp rule

WhatsApp must not be the main CTA on the website and must not be the hero CTA on the homepage.

For a premium marketing company, the main conversion path should remain professional: form, call, email, meeting, or audit request.

WhatsApp can exist as a secondary option for the Moroccan market, but it must stay discreet: text link, small secondary button on the Contact page, or footer mention.

### Button style

Avoid generic over-rounded pill buttons.

Prefer:

- premium rectangular buttons;
- slightly rounded corners;
- thin borders;
- sharp typography;
- subtle hover state;
- visible focus state;
- small technical detail if tasteful.

### Button variants

#### Primary

Main action. Force Blue or Soft White depending on contrast. Strong, clear, and precise.

#### Secondary

Transparent or dark background. Visible border. Clear text.

#### Ghost

Low-emphasis action for header, footer, or secondary sections.

#### Text link

Premium link with subtle underline or short arrow.

#### Small CTA

For cards, footer, and secondary actions.

## 15. Copywriting Rules

### Recommended message structure

AWF copy should follow this logic:

**Problem → System → Execution → Result**

Example:

> Une marque peut publier tous les jours et rester invisible commercialement. Le problème n’est pas toujours le volume. C’est souvent l’absence de système entre message, format, campagne et conversion. AWF construit ce système.

### Clarity rule

Every piece of copy should answer at least one of these questions:

- What problem are we solving?
- For whom?
- How?
- With what deliverables?
- Toward what result?
- What action should the reader take?

### CTA microcopy

Prefer:

- Demander un audit growth
- Planifier un échange
- Découvrir nos services
- Voir nos travaux
- Recevoir une recommandation
- Lancer un diagnostic

Avoid:

- Cliquez ici
- En savoir plus as the only CTA label
- Booster ma marque
- Démarrer l’aventure
- Parlons magie

## 20. Do & Don’t

### Do

- Use AWF as the main visible brand.
- Add “Marketing Workforce” to clarify the positioning.
- Keep a premium, dark, structured visual identity.
- Speak about marketing systems, not only posts.
- Connect content, campaigns, and conversion.
- Show methodology.
- Use professional CTAs.
- Keep WhatsApp as a discreet secondary option.
- Write with clarity and precision.
- Publish only validated results.

### Don’t

- Make AWF look like a recruitment company.
- Use WhatsApp as the primary hero CTA.
- Use generic over-rounded pill buttons.
- Use megaphones, rockets, headsets, or cheap icons.
- Use generic corporate stock photos.
- Say “boostez votre visibilité”.
- Promise unproven results.
- Overdecorate interfaces.
- Copy generic social media agency visual codes.
- Use too many emojis in corporate materials.
